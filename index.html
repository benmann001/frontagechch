FAQ

Questions businesses usually ask first.

These are the practical things we check before any serious install conversation.

Will it block the view into my store?

No, the goal is to use transparent display technology so your window can show digital content while still keeping a level of visibility and openness. The exact result depends on product type, brightness, pixel pitch and content design.

Is this only for big shopfronts?

No. Smaller feature windows can work well if the location, campaign and visibility are strong. A smaller, well-placed display can be more effective than a large display in the wrong location.

Do I need Council or landlord approval?

Possibly. Approval depends on the site, tenancy, display type, content, brightness and whether it is treated as signage. We flag approval considerations during the storefront review.

What content can we show?

Offers, menus, launches, new arrivals, appointment availability, seasonal campaigns, brand video, testimonials, QR codes, event promos and simple calls to action.

How much does it cost?

It depends on display size, technology, brightness needs, installation complexity and content support. The first step is a storefront review so we can give a realistic pathway instead of guessing from generic pricing.

Can you manage the content too?

Yes. The strongest version of this is not just installing hardware. It is keeping the storefront active with fresh campaign content, seasonal updates and high-quality visuals.
